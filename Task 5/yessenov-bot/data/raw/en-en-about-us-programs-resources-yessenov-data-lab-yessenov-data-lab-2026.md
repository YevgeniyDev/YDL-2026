Yessenov Data Lab 2026 is a 4-week intensive training program aimed at teaching young Kazakhstani scientists and professionals the basic skills of working with Big Data and artificial intelligence.

Dates of the school: June 15 to July 10, 2026 (Online: June 15–26, Offline in Almaty: June 29 – July 10).

Number of participants: 20

PROGRAM TERMS

Program participants: Citizens of the Republic of Kazakhstan over 18 years of age, undergraduate, master’s and doctoral students, as well as working professionals in technical and natural science fields.

Candidate requirements:

- strong analytical skills;

- interest and willingness to engage in scientific activities and/or develop IT technologies in Kazakhstan;

- basic knowledge of statistics and linear algebra;

- basic knowledge of programming (in any programming language).

The applicant must have a personal laptop with specifications sufficient for comfortable programming. Recommended parameters:

- CPU Intel Core Gen 7 and higher, Mac Silicon M1 and higher;

- RAM 6 GB and higher.

Language of instruction: Russian, with possible use of technical English

Submission deadline:

March 26 to April 28, 2026 (inclusive).

Fill out an application on the Foundation’s website and provide the following documents:

Mandatory documents:

- copy of ID;

- student transcript (GPA not lower than 3.0). Applications with a GPA lower than 3.0 will not be considered;

- certificate of no criminal record / presence of a criminal record;

- a copy of the transcript (all semesters) or undergraduate diploma with grades (for master’s students and professionals);

- an essay on the topic “I want to learn how to analyze data so that…” (not less than 7 sentences and no more than 750 words), submitted in an editable format and written in the language of the application form – Kazakh, Russian, or English;

- CV.

Additional documents:

A portfolio demonstrating the candidate’s achievements in scientific research and/or expressed interests in a specific scientific/technical area, with supporting documents:

- certificates for participation/victories in olympiads – no more than 2 certificates;

- certificates confirming completion of courses – no more than 2 certificates;

- certificates for social activity and volunteering – no more than 2 certificates;

- publications – no more than 2 publications;

- links to projects in which the candidate participated or implemented independently – no more than 3 examples (each may include up to 2 images/screenshots/links and a description of up to 150 words).

* The Foundation considers a limited number of documents. Candidates must independently select those that best reflect their achievements.

The deadline for application submission is April 28, 2026, at 3:00 PM (Astana time, GMT+5).

Applications submitted after the deadline will not be considered.
